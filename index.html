How It Works

This demonstration shows how a ship appears to sink below the horizon as it sails away from an observer. This effect is due to the curvature of the Earth.

The distance to the horizon depends on the observer's height above sea level. For an observer at 2 meters height, the horizon is about 5 kilometers away.

As the ship sails beyond the horizon, it gradually disappears from bottom to top. The hull disappears first, followed by the superstructure, and finally the masts and antennas.

Using the telescope view, you can see this effect more clearly. The telescope magnifies the view but cannot see beyond the horizon.

Atmospheric refraction can increase the visible distance by effectively increasing Earth's radius. Under standard conditions (k=1.33), visibility extends about 15% further than geometric calculations would suggest.
